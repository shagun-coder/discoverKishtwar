History & Mahabharata Connection üèõÔ∏è

Kuntwara holds a prominent place in the ancient lore of the Mahabharata. According to local traditions, this area is believed to have been visited by the Pandavas during their period of exile (Agyatvas). The valley provided shelter, fresh water, and strategic routes through the surrounding mountains, which were used by the Pandavas while evading their enemies.

Several ancient temples in Kuntwara are said to have been built to commemorate visits by the Pandavas or events related to the epic. These temples house intricately carved **stone sculptures**, many of which depict scenes from the Mahabharata, such as battles, sages offering guidance, and divine figures blessing the heroes. Local folklore suggests that these structures were established by the early settlers to preserve the memory of these sacred events.

The people of Kuntwara maintain traditional festivals, folk songs, and dance rituals connected to harvest cycles and seasonal events.

Handicrafts, local weaving, and terrace farming techniques are still preserved across villages.

Kuntwara consists of small, tightly-knit villages with populations ranging from a few hundred to a thousand residents. The communities follow a mix of agrarian and pastoral lifestyles.

The area experiences heavy snowfall in winter, making it a serene yet remote location.
