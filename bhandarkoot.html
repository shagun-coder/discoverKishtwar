Bhandarkot Valley: Scenic Splendor

A serene valley famous for natural beauty, river streams, and the Bhandarkoot Ziyarat shrine.

‚Üê Back to Places & Villages

Bhandarkoot (also spelt Bandarkoot) is a picturesque village in Kishtwar district, Jammu and Kashmir, India. It is located 12 km from Kishtwar town on the Kishtwar-Sinthan-Anantnag road. Bhandarkoot is famous for the Bhandarkoot Ziyarat, a holy shrine of Hazrat Sheikh Zain-ud-din Rishi. The village also marks the confluence of the Marusudar River, a tributary that joins the Chenab River here.

Geography & Rivers üåä

Bhandarkoot is located at the junction of the Marusudar River, the largest tributary of the Chenab River. The river valley is fertile and surrounded by hills, offering a scenic view and rich flora and fauna.

Natural Features

  • Marusudar River confluence
  • Fertile valley and surrounding hills
  • Forests of deodar, pine, and oak

Culture & Shrine üïå

Bhandarkoot is famous for the Bhandarkoot Ziyarat, attracting devotees from across the region. The local lifestyle revolves around agriculture, animal husbandry, and traditional festivals.

Highlights

  • Annual pilgrimages to Bhandarkoot Ziyarat
  • Traditional farming and lifestyle
  • Local handicrafts and festivals

History & Significance üèõ

Bhandarkoot has been historically important as a junction connecting remote valleys of Kishtwar and as a stop for pilgrims and traders traveling toward Kashmir or Kishtwar town.
